Alzumal

Owner & Software Developer

In late 2022, I envisioned a more efficient method of performing lighting calculations: a method which would make use of data in the BIM model as well as the nascient ray-tracing extensions made available to Nvidia graphics cards through the Vulkan API. Ray-tracing capable cards are set to become commonplace in the building design industry over the next few years.

Over the following months, I set about creating the business structure to market the software and began developing a proof-of-concept in earnest. After a few misguided attempts in early 2023, I finally developed a workable proof-of-concept for real-time point-by-point illuminance calculations around October 2023. Progress was stalled for months due to holidays and a loss of momentum. I also became involved in a large middle school addition project at Southern A&E.

As I progressed to the meaty part of software development full of high impact decisions to make, I was unable to make forward progress and maintain any semblance of a healthy lifestyle. I informed my manager that I would see the middle school addition project through the final deliverable date and then submit my 2 weeks notice.

On April 25th, 2024, I submitted my 2 weeks notice and on May 9th, 2024 I resigned and sold off my shares of the firm.

After a brief respite, I continued development of the add-in. The initial beta version of the add-in was released to a small audience of beta program participants on June 14th, 2024. Development of beta releases continues today.

If you would like to participate in the beta program, please contact me and I will ensure you have compatible hardware before sending you an installer and support files.
